Peach Cake Recipe

Indulge in the delightful taste of summer with our Peach Cake, a moist and flavorful treat that perfectly captures the essence of ripe, juicy peaches. This cake combines the tender crumb of a classic vanilla cake with the sweet, tangy goodness of fresh peaches, creating a harmonious blend of flavors and textures.

Each bite of this cake offers a burst of peachy sweetness, complemented by a hint of vanilla and a soft, buttery texture. The addition of diced peaches throughout the batter ensures that every slice is filled with juicy pockets of fruit, making it an irresistible dessert for any occasion.

Topped with a light peach glaze or a dusting of powdered sugar, this cake is not only delicious but also beautifully presented. Whether enjoyed as a simple afternoon treat or served at a special gathering, our Peach Cake is sure to impress with its vibrant flavor and comforting, homemade charm

Peach Cake Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 ½ cups fresh peaches, peeled and diced
  • Optional: ¼ cup peach preserves for glaze

How to Bake a Cake

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 350°F (175°C) and let it preheat while you prepare the cake batter.
  2. Prepare the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour, 1 cup of sugar, ½ cup of softened butter, 2 large eggs, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, 1 ½ teaspoons of baking powder, and ½ cup of milk. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  3. Grease and Flour the Pan: Grease a 9-inch round cake pan with butter or non-stick spray, then lightly flour it to prevent sticking.
  4. Pour and Bake: Pour the cake batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Cool and Serve: Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before frosting or serving.
